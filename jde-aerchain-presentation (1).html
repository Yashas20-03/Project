54% of POs are ZSER (service type) on cost centre with free-text descriptions — this is why AI-driven intake and category mapping is critical. No material code to anchor to.

Requester Pain Points
P-01 · REQUESTER
SAP is too complex for non-procurement users
Cost centres, GL accounts, material groups, purchasing organisations — errors are common, causing invoice blocks downstream. Requesters avoid the system entirely or raise incorrect PRs.
P-02 · REQUESTER
No visibility of preferred suppliers or negotiated prices at intake
Requesters select suppliers independently with no awareness of preferred vendor lists or contracted rates, leading to maverick spend and compliance failures.
P-03 · REQUESTER
No single entry point — fragmented by region, category and habit
Email in Europe, SharePoint in APAC and Brazil, MS Teams file uploads in IKEMIA. Zero consistency, no demand visibility, and no audit trail.
Buyer / Procurement Pain Points
P-04 · BUYER
Procurement involved too late — leverage already lost
Supplier is already selected and commercial terms agreed informally before procurement is engaged. Contract leverage is minimal, and compliance is an afterthought.
P-05 · BUYER
Low-value admin consumes strategic sourcing capacity
SAP assistance, chasing GRNs, and correcting incorrectly raised PRs consume buyer bandwidth that should be directed at category strategy and supplier relationships.
AP / Finance / Operations Pain Points
P-06 · AP/FINANCE
High rate of backdated POs and invoice blocks
Europe sees a persistently high backdated PO rate. Invoices arrive without GRNs, causing payment delays, supplier escalations, and 3-way match failures in SAP ECC.
P-07 · OPERATIONS
MRO requisition process is manual and reactive
Plant engineers manage 100–150 SAP MRP ZNB requisitions per day manually. No marketplace-first routing, no consolidated view across plants, no engineer-friendly validation UI.
P-08 · COMPLIANCE
No-PO-No-Pay policy violations remain unmeasured
Policy compliance is currently unmeasured. GRN posting is invoice-triggered (reactive) rather than delivery-triggered (proactive). No automated enforcement mechanism exists.
